This Privacy Policy explains how TechNext Pte. Ltd. collects, uses, discloses, and safeguards your personal data in accordance with Singapore's Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (PDPA). By using our website or services, you consent to the practices described in this Policy.

1 Overview

TechNext Pte. Ltd. ("TechNext", "we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of personal data that we collect from clients, prospects, website visitors, and business partners. This Policy applies to all personal data processed by us in connection with our business operations, including our website at technext.asia and all related services.

We only collect personal data that is necessary for the purposes set out in this Policy and process it lawfully, fairly, and transparently.

5 Legal Basis for Processing

Under Singapore's PDPA, we rely on the following grounds to process your personal data:

  • Consent — where you have given us clear, informed consent (e.g. newsletter sign-up, booking forms)
  • Contract — where processing is necessary to perform a contract with you or to take steps at your request prior to entering a contract
  • Legitimate Interest — where we have a legitimate business interest that is not overridden by your privacy rights (e.g. fraud prevention, website analytics)
  • Legal Obligation — where we are required to process data to comply with applicable law

6 Sharing Your Personal Data

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We may share your data only in the following circumstances:

6.1 Service Providers

We work with trusted third-party providers who process data on our behalf, including:

  • Cloud hosting providers (e.g. Amazon Web Services, Google Cloud)
  • CRM and helpdesk platforms (e.g. Odoo, Zoho)

6.3 Legal Requirements

6.4 Cross-Border Transfers

As we operate in Singapore, Vietnam, and the Philippines, your data may be transferred to and processed in these countries. Where data is transferred outside Singapore, we ensure comparable protection is in place through contractual safeguards consistent with PDPA requirements.

8 Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law:

  • Client data — retained for 7 years after the end of a contract (IRAS/ACRA requirements)
  • Enquiry and consultation data — retained for 2 years from the date of last contact
  • Marketing opt-in records — retained until you unsubscribe or withdraw consent
  • Website analytics data — anonymised after 26 months
  • Job applicant data — retained for 1 year if not hired (with consent)

Once the retention period expires, we securely delete or anonymise your personal data.

9 Your Rights

Under Singapore's PDPA, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  1. Right of Access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  2. Right of Correction — request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data
  3. Right of Withdrawal — withdraw consent at any time (this does not affect processing carried out before withdrawal)
  4. Right to Data Portability — request your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  5. Right to Erasure — request deletion of your data where there is no legitimate reason to continue processing
  6. Right to Object — object to processing for direct marketing purposes

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our DPO at dpo@technext.asia. We will respond to all requests within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before processing your request.

If you are dissatisfied with our response, you may lodge a complaint with the Personal Data Protection Commission (PDPC) at www.pdpc.gov.sg.

10 Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, accidental loss, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These include:

  • TLS/SSL encryption for all data transmitted to and from our website
  • Access controls and role-based permissions for staff handling personal data
  • Regular security assessments and penetration testing
  • Staff training on data protection and security practices
  • Secure data centres with physical access controls and redundancy

In the event of a data breach that is likely to result in significant harm, we will notify the PDPC and affected individuals in accordance with the PDPA Mandatory Data Breach Notification requirements.
