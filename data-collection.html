This Data Collection Statement provides a granular, transparent account of exactly what personal data TechNext Pte. Ltd. collects, how and why we collect it, the legal basis for each category, and the rights you hold under Singapore's Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (No. 26 of 2012), as amended by the PDPA (Amendment) Act 2020. It should be read alongside our Privacy Policy.

1 Purpose of This Statement

TechNext Pte. Ltd. ("TechNext", "we", "us", "our") is committed to full transparency in how we handle personal data. While our Privacy Policy provides a broad overview, this Data Collection Statement is designed to give you a more detailed, practical picture of every category of personal data we collect — what it is, how it is gathered, why it is needed, and how long we keep it.

This Statement is issued in accordance with the following instruments of Singapore law:

  • Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (No. 26 of 2012), as amended by the PDPA (Amendment) Act 2020
  • PDPC Advisory Guidelines on the Collection, Use and Disclosure of Personal Data
  • PDPC Advisory Guidelines on Notification (Data Breach Notification)
  • Do Not Call Provisions (Part IX, PDPA)
  • PDPC's Guide to Data Protection Practices for ICT Systems

If you have questions at any time, please contact our Data Protection Officer at dpo@technext.asia.

2 Who This Applies To

This Statement applies to all individuals whose personal data is collected or processed by TechNext Pte. Ltd., including:

  • Website visitors — anyone who accesses technext.asia or any associated subdomains
  • Prospective clients — individuals who submit enquiry forms, book a free ERP consultation, or download resources from our website
  • Existing clients and their employees — individuals whose data is processed as part of an active service contract
  • Business contacts — individuals whose contact details we receive through referrals, trade events, or professional networks
  • Job applicants — candidates who apply for positions at TechNext through any channel
  • Suppliers and partners — individuals acting on behalf of organisations with which TechNext has a business relationship

This Statement does not apply to the personal data of our employees, which is governed by our internal Human Resources Data Protection Policy.

3 Categories of Personal Data Collected

We collect only personal data that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary for the stated purposes. The categories we may collect include:

3.1 Identity & Contact Data

  • Full name and preferred name
  • Business email address and personal email address (where voluntarily provided)
  • Phone number or mobile number
  • Job title and department
  • LinkedIn profile URL (where shared)

3.2 Organisation Data

  • Company name and UEN (where applicable)
  • Industry sector and company size
  • Country and city of operation
  • ERP readiness information and current software stack

3.3 Technical & Usage Data

  • IP address (partial or full)
  • Browser type, version, and language settings
  • Operating system and device type
  • Pages visited, time on page, scroll depth, and click patterns
  • Referring URL and UTM parameters
  • Session duration and bounce indicators

3.4 Cookie & Preference Data

  • Cookie identifiers (session and persistent)
  • Display preferences (e.g. dark/light mode setting)
  • Analytics identifiers assigned by Google Analytics or similar tools
  • Advertising identifiers for retargeting (where consent is given)

3.5 Contract & Transaction Data

  • Invoicing name and billing address
  • Project scope and service agreement terms
  • Payment status records (we do not store payment card numbers)
  • IRAS-relevant transaction data for accounting and tax compliance

3.6 Communication Records

  • Email correspondence (sent and received)
  • Live chat and support ticket transcripts
  • Video call notes and meeting minutes (with consent where applicable)
  • Feedback, complaints, and dispute-related communications

3.7 Marketing Preference Data

  • Newsletter opt-in status and date of consent
  • Communication channel preferences (email, WhatsApp, phone)
  • Content interest tags (e.g. Odoo modules, ERP best practices)
  • Unsubscribe records and consent withdrawal timestamps

3.8 Recruitment Data

  • CV or résumé (including work history, education, and skills)
  • Cover letter content
  • Interview notes and assessment scores
  • Right-to-work documentation (where legally required)
We do not collect sensitive personal data such as NRIC/FIN numbers, passport numbers, health or medical information, racial or ethnic origin, religious beliefs, or financial account credentials unless there is a specific, disclosed legal or contractual requirement and we have obtained explicit consent.

4 How We Collect Your Data

We collect personal data through the following methods:

4.1 Web Forms (Direct Submission)

When you complete any form on our website — including the free ERP consultation booking form, contact form, newsletter subscription form, or resource download form — we collect the data you voluntarily submit. Mandatory fields are marked; optional fields help us personalise your experience but are not required.

4.2 Cookies & Automated Tracking Technologies

When you visit technext.asia, cookies and similar technologies automatically collect technical and behavioural data. This includes analytics cookies (Google Analytics), functionality cookies (dark mode preference), and, where you consent, advertising cookies. See Section 7 for a full breakdown.

4.3 Analytics Platforms

We use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to understand how visitors interact with our website. GA4 collects anonymised, aggregated data including page views, session duration, geographic region (country/city level), and device type. IP addresses are anonymised before storage. We do not use GA4 for individual profiling.

4.4 Direct Communication

When you contact us by email, phone, live chat, or social media messaging, we retain a record of that communication including your contact details and the content of your message. This is used to respond to your enquiry and maintain service continuity.

4.5 Third-Party Referrals & Professional Networks

We may receive your contact details from Odoo's partner referral programme, business directories, LinkedIn, or professional contacts who believe our services may be relevant to you. In all cases, we verify that the referral is legitimate and ensure you are informed of how your data reached us at the earliest practical opportunity.

4.6 Business Cards & Events

When you share your business card with us at trade exhibitions, networking events, or in-person meetings, we may digitise and store your contact details for the purpose of following up on our conversation. You will be given a clear opportunity to opt out of further communications at any time.

6 Do Not Call (DNC) Registry Compliance

TechNext Pte. Ltd. fully complies with the Do Not Call Provisions (Part IX, Personal Data Protection Act 2012). Before making any telemarketing call or sending any telemarketing text message to a Singapore telephone number, we check that number against the DNC Registry maintained by the Personal Data Protection Commission (PDPC).

6.1 What Counts as a Telemarketing Message

Under the PDPA, a "specified message" is any voice call, text message (SMS/MMS), or fax that advertises or promotes the supply of goods or services, the sale or lease of land, or a business opportunity. TechNext does not send specified messages to any Singapore telephone number that appears on the DNC Registry without the clear prior consent of the recipient.

6.2 Consent-Based Outreach

Where you have provided your telephone number and expressly indicated that you consent to receiving calls or messages from TechNext (for example, by selecting the relevant option on our consultation booking form), we rely on that consent. Your consent is logged with a timestamp and may be withdrawn at any time by contacting us at dpo@technext.asia or by replying STOP to any message.

6.3 Existing Business Relationship

Where we have an ongoing or recent business relationship with you, we may contact you by telephone or text for the purpose of facilitating or completing that transaction, even if your number is registered on the DNC. Such contact will be limited strictly to the relevant service engagement.

If you believe you have received an unsolicited telemarketing communication from TechNext, please report it immediately to dpo@technext.asia or call us at +65 8427 2549. We take DNC compliance seriously and will investigate all complaints promptly.

8 Cross-Border Data Transfers

TechNext Pte. Ltd. is headquartered in Singapore and operates delivery teams in Vietnam and the Philippines. Personal data collected in Singapore may be transferred to and processed in these countries as part of our service delivery operations.

8.1 Countries Involved

  • Singapore — primary data controller; all data governance decisions are made here
  • Vietnam (Ho Chi Minh City) — technical implementation and support delivery team
  • Philippines (BGC, Taguig) — customer success and project coordination team

8.2 Safeguards in Place

In accordance with Section 26 of the PDPA, before transferring personal data to a country outside Singapore, TechNext ensures that the recipient provides a standard of protection at least comparable to that under the PDPA. The safeguards we rely on include:

  • Intra-group data transfer agreements incorporating PDPC-aligned contractual clauses
  • Data processing agreements with all sub-processors that impose equivalent obligations
  • Access controls that restrict overseas team members to the minimum data necessary for their function
  • Regular audits of our overseas offices' data handling practices

8.3 Third-Party Cloud Infrastructure

We use cloud services from providers including Amazon Web Services (AWS) and Google Cloud. Data may be stored in servers located in the Asia-Pacific region. All such providers are bound by contractual data protection obligations and are certified under internationally recognised security standards (e.g. ISO 27001, SOC 2). We select server regions in Asia-Pacific (Singapore, Tokyo, or Sydney) wherever possible to minimise data transfer distance.

If you would like details of the specific safeguards in place for any cross-border transfer affecting your personal data, please contact our DPO at dpo@technext.asia.

9 Data Breach Notification

Singapore's Mandatory Data Breach Notification obligation (effective 1 February 2021, introduced by the PDPA (Amendment) Act 2020, Sections 26C–26D) requires organisations to notify both the PDPC and affected individuals when a data breach occurs that meets specified thresholds. TechNext has implemented procedures to detect, assess, and respond to data breaches promptly.

9.1 Notification to the PDPC

Where a data breach is assessed as being likely to result in significant harm to any affected individual, or where it affects 500 or more individuals, we are required to notify the PDPC as soon as practicable and in any event within 3 calendar days of assessing that the breach meets the notification threshold.

9.2 Notification to Affected Individuals

Where a breach is assessed as being likely to result in significant harm to a specific individual (for example, identity fraud, financial loss, physical harm, or significant reputational damage), we will notify that individual as soon as practicable. Notification will be provided:

  • By email to the address we hold on record, and/or
  • By telephone or written notice if email is unavailable or inadequate
  • By means of a prominent notice on our website in cases where direct notification is not possible

The notification will include a description of the nature of the breach, the types of data involved, the likely consequences, the measures we have taken or propose to take, and the contact details of our DPO.

9.3 Our Internal Response Process

TechNext maintains a Data Breach Response Plan that sets out the steps our team must take upon discovering a potential breach. This includes immediate containment, a formal impact assessment within 24 hours, escalation to our DPO, and coordination with our legal advisors where required. We conduct post-incident reviews to prevent recurrence.

If you become aware of or suspect a data breach involving your personal data held by TechNext, please contact our DPO immediately at dpo@technext.asia or call +65 8427 2549. Early notification helps us act faster to contain any harm.

10 Your Rights Under the PDPA

Under Singapore's Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (as amended), you have the following rights in relation to your personal data held by TechNext. To exercise any of these rights, please submit a written request to our DPO at dpo@technext.asia. We will acknowledge your request within 5 business days and provide a full response within 30 calendar days (or notify you if more time is needed, up to a maximum of 60 days under the PDPA).

  1. Right of Access — You may request confirmation of whether we hold personal data about you, and if so, a copy of that data and information about how it is being used. We may charge a reasonable fee for processing complex or repeated requests.
  2. Right of Correction — You may request that we correct any personal data about you that is inaccurate, incomplete, misleading, or out of date. We will make corrections within 30 days and, where appropriate, inform third parties to whom the data was disclosed.
  3. Right to Withdraw Consent — Where we rely on your consent as the legal basis for processing, you may withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal. After withdrawal, we will cease the relevant processing unless another legal basis applies.
  4. Right to Data Portability — You may request that we provide your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format (e.g. CSV or JSON) so that you can transfer it to another organisation. This right applies to data you have provided to us directly and which is processed on the basis of consent or contract.
  5. Right to Erasure — You may request deletion of your personal data where it is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, where you have withdrawn consent and there is no other legal basis, or where the data has been processed unlawfully. We will comply unless we are required to retain the data by law (e.g. IRAS records for 7 years).
  6. Right to Object — You may object at any time to the processing of your personal data for direct marketing purposes. Upon receipt of your objection, we will immediately cease using your data for that purpose. You may also object to processing based on legitimate interest, in which case we will balance your interests against ours.
All rights requests should be sent to dpo@technext.asia with the subject line "PDPA Rights Request". Please include your full name, the email address associated with your data, and a description of the data or action concerned. We may ask you to verify your identity before we process the request.

12 Contact & Complaints

If you have any questions, concerns, or complaints about this Data Collection Statement or about the way TechNext handles your personal data, please contact us in the first instance using the details in Section 11. We take all complaints seriously and will investigate promptly.

12.1 Internal Complaints Procedure

Upon receiving your complaint:

  1. We will acknowledge receipt within 5 business days.
  2. Our DPO will conduct a thorough investigation and may contact you to request further information.
  3. We will provide a substantive written response within 30 calendar days of receiving all necessary information.
  4. If you remain dissatisfied, you may escalate to the PDPC (see below).

12.2 Escalation to the PDPC

If you are not satisfied with our response, or if you believe we have failed to comply with our obligations under the PDPA, you may lodge a complaint with the Personal Data Protection Commission (PDPC):

  • Online: www.pdpc.gov.sg → "File a complaint"
  • Address: 10 Pasir Panjang Road, #03-01 Mapletree Business City, Singapore 117438
  • Helpline: +65 6377 3131

The PDPC has the authority to investigate complaints, require TechNext to take corrective action, and impose financial penalties for serious breaches of the PDPA.
